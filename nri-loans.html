FAQ

NRI loan questions

Do I need to visit India to apply for an NRI loan?

No. Most banks allow the entire process digitally. You'll need to send a notarised Power of Attorney to a trusted person in India for signing final documents. FLOCRED's team guides you through the POA process step by step.

What documents are needed for an NRI home loan?

Overseas: Valid passport, visa, work permit, last 3 months' salary slips, 6-month overseas bank statement, employment contract. India-side: Property documents, PAN card, NRE/NRO account details. ARIA gives you the exact list for your specific lender.

Can I repay the NRI loan from my overseas account?

Yes. EMIs can be paid from your NRE (Non-Resident External) or NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account. HDFC and SBI accept direct international remittances. Repayment in foreign currency is FEMA-compliant.

What is the maximum loan an NRI can get?

Home Loan: Up to ₹5 Crore (some banks higher for high-income NRIs). LAP: Up to ₹10 Crore. The amount depends on your overseas income, the property value, and the lender's NRI policy. ARIA calculates your maximum in 2 minutes.
